Here are three useful ideas for commercial property owners and property owners in the consequences of flood damage:

Suggestion # 1 - Check the structure for leakages in the pipes lines, roofing, and siding right now. Search for evidence of water entering the structure. Undetected, mold issues may intensify till the point where aggressive remediation is the only recourse. With timely detection, however, sometimes the only response needed is extensive cleansing, drying, and perhaps some minor repair work to the structure.

Tip # 2 - Engage a mold expert to do a comprehensive inspection of the building. Porous building products are especially susceptible to harm from absorbing water and any impurities in that water. Mold spores start germination within only a couple of days and can rapidly cause a major issue. A mold specialist has the technology and training to assess visible and hidden signs of water intrusion and mold growth throughout the whole structure, consisting of every room on every floor, crawl spaces and basements, HVAC ducts, inside and out. You will not just get a written mold assessment, but also recommendations for remedying any concerns found.

Suggestion # 3 - If the mold inspection exposes any mold problems, take remediation action immediately. While it might be appealing to try to hide or camouflage water damage with paint, the smell will quickly be unmistakable and the issue will not go away on its own. Worse yet, the indoor air quality will end up being increasingly unpleasant and unsafe for the building's residents. Failing to remediate leaves the building owners and managers susceptible to suits, and is never worth the risk.
